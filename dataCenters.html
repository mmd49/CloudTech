Data Centers

Data Centers and the Cloud

Datacenters are clusters of computers and storage systems with a vast power supply infrastructure and data communication connections. Due to its mission-critical nature, a data center must have backup components (in the form of disk arrays) for data failover as well as battery banks and generators for electrical power redundancy. Environmental controls for temperature and humidity regulation and fire suppression are also deployed.

A data center holds enterprise applications and data for many users, which means security is a vital component of the system architecture. The cloud can be thought of as software and services running on the Internet, instead of on your local computer.This feat of technology is accomplished through the interconnection of global datacenters using ultra-highspeed optical connections with massive bandwidth.

The advantage of the cloud is that you can access your information on any device from any location with an Internet connection, allowing users to collaborate on the same document simultaneously. The cloud also removes the performance and storage limitations of the local machine because the data center servers handle the bulk of the processing and data storage. However, without a strong Internet connection, the cloud loses its advantages. And online data storage in the cloud is inherently riskier than storage on your local machine.
